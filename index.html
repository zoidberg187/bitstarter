A tontine is a spin on traditional investing. In a tontine, a group of people pool their money together in an investment over a period of time, for example 20 years. If any of the members dies before the investment term ends then their share is split among the remaining members. Our goal is to provide secure tontine-style investments by leveraging the anonymousness of the internet. Anonymous Tontine participants are not incentivized to harm one another since their identities are unknown.

Hundreds of millions of Americans already invest in similar strategies with life insurance. Through our “Mutual Beneficiary” program, we aim to deliver huge returns to our investors by tapping into the existing life insurance infrastructure.

If your goal is growth then our service is perfect for you. No other investment strategy can compete with the low risk and huge returns offered by Anonymous Tontine.

FAQ

Is this legal?

It's typically outlawed in the modern world becuase participants are incentivized to murder each other for larger payouts. See episodes of "The Simpsons" and "Archer" for examples in today's pop culture.

Our service is different because there is no incentive to harm other participants. Still, we do need to pursue legal clearance.

Do I need to have a life insurance policy in order to participate?
No, but among our several investment options we figure that the "mutual beneficiary" programs will be some of the most lucrative.
